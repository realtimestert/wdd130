So Now You're Having Trouble?

Some Tips and Pointers

Here are some questions to ask yourself when planning a LAN party.

  • What happens when a player is kicked out of a multi-hour game?
  • Should there just be one game or many games to play?
  • What time constraints are there? Or in other words, how long will it be?
  • What should be done about the body odor situation?
  • How hot are things going to get with all the people and hot computers in a small space?
  • When should gaming time end? You need to remember that people need food and sleep.
  • Remember to take breaks. Don't overdo it.
  • What are you going to do when somebody is a sore loser/winner?
  • Unfortunately, many people learned bad online etiquette while playing online games. People need to understand that at a close quarters party like this there needs to be an understanding of what to do when you win or lose. No bullying.
  • Can you lose gracefully?

There is more to do than hoping everything will just plug and play without problems

  • Will this just be a party between friends and/or family?
  • How much seating will there be?
  • What will you do when more people than you expected showed up?
  • Do you need a sign-up sheet to make sure that not too many people show up?
  • Lots of computers will mean a greater power draw.
  • Remember that some people will only be able to spend a little bit of money.
  • That is why free games are always an option.
  • You could also be generous and help them buy a cheap game that everyone is going to play, but be careful. Doing this too much can drain your wallet!
